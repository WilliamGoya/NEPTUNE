What Neptune does

🚢

Fleet visibility

Live AIS positions for every commercial vessel in your area of operations, with type, flag, dimensions, and current navigational status.

⚓

Port coordination

See which vessels are at anchor, moored, or arriving at major Mediterranean ports. Reduce coordination friction between fleet operators and port authorities.

📊

Fleet analytics

Composition by type and flag, navigational status distribution, fleet capacity totals — the dataset behind every operational decision.

🤖

AI ops assistant

Natural-language access to the entire dataset. Ask "where is MSC OSCAR?", "how many tankers?", or "what's at Algeciras?" and get instant answers.

🔔

Watchlist alerts

Track specific vessels by name or MMSI. Neptune surfaces them as soon as they appear on the AIS network.

⚡

Real-time data

WebSocket-driven AIS ingestion with sub-minute latency. Built on Flask, Postgres, and async Python.

Who it's for

Commercial ports

Berth allocation teams, port authorities, and harbor masters who need to coordinate dozens of arrivals per day across cargo, tanker, and passenger traffic.

Fleet operators

Merchant marine companies tracking ETAs, fuel costs, and port-call efficiency across their fleet — without depending on phone calls or fragmented vendor portals.

Service providers

Bunkering, pilotage, towage, and provisioning agents who need real-time vessel status to plan their own operations.
